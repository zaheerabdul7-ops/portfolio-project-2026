## Post 1 — June 11, 2026

I've worked at 10 companies, and performance reviews have ranged from 
mildly useful to an utter circus.

Part of the problem is frequency: When reviews happen once a year, 
people mostly remember the last project you worked on. When they happen 
every week, the feedback gets very tactical, and there is not enough 
time to actually apply it and improve.

Part of it is also quality: How much can you really coach someone if 
you don't have clear examples of what happened and when?

As a founder, I used to keep one Google Doc per direct report and add 
notes whenever I noticed something during the week. That kind of 
worked... But man, that became way too much admin, way too quickly.

So I just stopped doing it. That gave me a lot of time back and mental 
space, but it also meant a lot of growth opportunities were missed for 
my direct reports.

Now that I'm working on a business operating system, I can change that.

**Question to audience:** Do you have a system for capturing ongoing 
feedback, or is it still mostly memory plus last-minute review prep?

## Key Insight
Performance systems and outbound systems share the same problem —
without structure and data, both break down. Jeremy applies operational
thinking to cold outreach the same way.

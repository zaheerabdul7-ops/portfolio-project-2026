## Post 1 — June 25, 2026

Call me old fashioned.

But after running a lot of LinkedIn outreach campaigns, I think almost 
every campaign can be improved with one thing:

A thoughtfully written intro DM.

Before you pitch. Before you talk about pain points. Before you mention 
social proof, case studies, or ROI.

Show your prospect you've actually learned something about them.

Me? I like using our "CCQ formula" for writing good openers:

- Compliment.
- Commonality.  
- Question.

A few examples:
- "Curious what you thought of Sam Smith's keynote last week?"
- "Caught your interview on IndustryPros."

So many people treat outbound like a pure numbers game that they forget 
the person on the other side has a steel-enforced spam filter.

You wouldn't walk around a bar proposing marriage until someone says yes.
So why start your first DM with a pitch?

Slow it down. I think you'll find it speeds up your results.

## Key Insight
CCQ Formula = Compliment + Commonality + Question
Use this before any pitch in LinkedIn outreach.

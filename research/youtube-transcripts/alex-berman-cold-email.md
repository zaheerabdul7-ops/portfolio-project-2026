## Core Framework

### 1. Relevance Beats Volume
Stop blasting untargeted lists. Every email must be tied to
something the prospect actually cares about.

### 2. Build a Narrow List
Bad target: "Marketing Directors"
Good target: "Marketing Directors at B2B SaaS, 20-100 employees,
raised Series A in last 18 months"
Stricter filters = more replies.

### 3. Intent Signals — Prioritize These
- Funding announcements
- New job postings
- Leadership transitions
- Office expansions
Companies showing these signals are changing fast and tend to respond.

### 4. Email Infrastructure (Non-Negotiable)
- Never send from main domain — buy 3-5 variation domains
- Set up SPF, DKIM, DMARC on every domain (15 mins per domain)
- Warm up domains: start 10-20 emails/day, increase to 50-80/day
- Keep bounce rate below 2%
- Tools: Instantly, SmartLead, Lemlist, GMass

### 5. Email Copy Formula — PC Formula
**Pain + Call to Action**
- Open with something specific to them
- Name the problem they're dealing with
- Explain in ONE sentence how you solve it
- Ask for one thing
- Total length: 50-125 words

Example:
"Noticed you haven't filled your digital sales manager role yet?
I run a cold email automation tool that can make that role irrelevant
and flood your calendar with meetings. Want me to send more info?"

### 6. Subject Lines — 3-7 Words Sweet Spot
- Short, specific, curiosity-driven
- Avoid: Free, Guarantee, Limited Time, Act Now
- Rotate 5-10 variations across campaign
- Test: 50 contacts version A vs 50 contacts version B

### 7. Follow-Up Sequence
- Day 0: Email 1
- Day 3: Email 2
- Day 7: Email 3
- Day 14: Email 4
Each follow-up needs a DIFFERENT angle — not just "bumping this up"
